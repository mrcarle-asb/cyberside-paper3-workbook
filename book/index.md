# CyberSide Paper 3 — Podcast Companions

Two short units to give you real-world context for the **IB CS 2026 Paper 3 case study**, *An ethical approach to hacking*. The case study drops you into a fictional pen-testing engagement at a hospital. These podcasts are from people who actually do this work for a living.

## The two units

1. **Ransomware Gangs (Ep. 59)** — *Cyberside Chats* on how modern ransomware groups operate. This is your **hook** — it connects to the Rockstar ransomware news from this weekend, and it builds the adversary vocabulary you'll need for Phase 3 (Threat modelling) of the case study.

2. **Pentesting (Ep. 67)** — Two working pen-testers walk through on-site social engineering: copier repairman pretexts, tailgating, OSINT, rubber duckies, the works. This is your **technique reference** — almost every case study terminology term shows up in the wild.

## How to use these pages

- Open a unit, hit **play** on the audio player at the top.
- Read along with the excerpts / frames as the conversation unfolds.
- **Type your answers directly into the text boxes** below each question.
- Your answers are automatically saved in **your own browser** — they'll still be there next class as long as you're on the same computer and don't clear your browser data.

:::alert{info}
**Your answers are local to your browser.** Nothing is submitted anywhere automatically. For turn-in, the pentesting unit has a **Bundle my answers** button at the bottom — click it, copy the text, paste into a Google Doc.
:::
